How to taste olive oil slowly

March 2025

Good olive oil announces itself quietly. You notice the aroma first, subtle hints of green or ripe fruit…

Bitterness is not a flaw — it’s a sign of freshness and quality. Take small sips, let the oil coat your tongue, and notice the heat in the throat.

Slowing down isn’t just for tasting — it trains your senses to notice the differences between oils from different regions, harvests, and presses.
