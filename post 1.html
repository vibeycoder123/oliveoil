How to taste olive oil slowly

March 2025

Tasting olive oil is an experience, not just a step in cooking. Start by pouring a small amount into a cup or spoon and bring it gently to your nose. Notice the aroma: fruity, grassy, nutty, or peppery.

Take a small sip, let the oil coat your tongue, and swallow slowly. Pay attention to bitterness and the slight peppery finish — these are signs of freshness and quality.

Compare different oils side by side to learn their subtle differences. Over time, your palate will recognize regions, harvests, and varieties.

Remember, tasting is about slowing down and enjoying every layer of flavor. Olive oil deserves this quiet attention, much like a fine wine.
